# Content Governance

## Approved versus Draft Content

All content published on Navpahal is assigned one of the following statuses:

- **Approved** — Confirmed organizational information that has been reviewed and authorized for publication.
- **Draft** — Content under development that has not been formally reviewed.
- **Proposed** — Suggested content that awaits organizational approval.
- **Awaiting Verification** — Content that requires independent verification before publication.
- **Under Development** — Sections or features that are planned but not yet built.

Only content with the **Approved** status should be treated as confirmed organizational information.

## Required Evidence for Quantitative Claims

No quantitative claims (statistics, percentages, beneficiary counts, impact metrics, or performance figures) may be published without:

1. A documented data collection methodology
2. Independent verification of the data
3. Organizational approval of the specific claim
4. A publication date and review period

All published metrics must include their source, methodology, date collected, and verification status.

## Approval Requirement for Contacts, Partners, and Testimonials

- **Contact details** (addresses, phone numbers, email addresses) require organizational approval before publication.
- **Partner organizations** listed on the website require explicit written consent from the partner and organizational approval.
- **Testimonials** require documented consent from the individual providing the testimonial, including confirmation that they understand how their statement will be used.

## Media Ownership and Consent Requirements

- All images, videos, and other media published on the website must be:
  - Owned by Navpahal, OR
  - Licensed for use (with documented license terms), OR
  - Used with explicit written consent from the creator or subject
- Media depicting individuals requires model release forms
- Media depicting children requires parental or guardian consent

## Content Approval Authority

Content approval is an organizational responsibility. Approval must be documented and traceable. The organization designates authorized approvers who are responsible for verifying content accuracy, appropriateness, and compliance with this governance framework.

No specific individual is named in this document as approver; the designation of approvers is an internal organizational process.

## Process for Correcting Published Information

1. **Identification** — Any person may identify content that appears inaccurate or outdated.
2. **Reporting** — Reports should be directed through the organizational contact process.
3. **Review** — Reported content is reviewed by an authorized approver.
4. **Correction** — If the content is found to be inaccurate, it is corrected or removed within a reasonable timeframe.
5. **Notification** — Significant corrections are communicated where the original content was published.
6. **Record** — Correction records are maintained for audit purposes.
